The Problem

Anastomosis is the joining of two tubular tissue sections, and is common in many surgeries. The quality of the anastomosis is critical to the success of these operations. However, leaks often develop in the anastomosis and seriously compromise health and recovery of the patients. Leaks also present a multi-million dollar expense on the healthcare system.

The Solution: Acustent

Acustent integrates the latest microneedle technology with a self-expanding stent to create an innovative support device for leak prevention in an anastomosis. By tackling the problem from multiple perspectives, the Acustent provides the additional protection that every anastomosis needs.

Specifications

Self-expanding Stent

Components and Parameters:

  • Thirty-six type 302 stainless steel torsion springs per stent
  • Cylindrical geometry
  • Resting Dimensions: 75 mm length; 55 mm diameter

Elastic Outer Wrap

Components and Parameters:

  • 0.006" Latex film
  • Tensile Strength: 3850 psi

Microneedles

Components and Parameters:

  • Liquid Plastic material
  • Fabricated from SmoothCast 300 - ultra-low viscosity casting resin
  • Cone-shaped needles
  • 3x20 microneedles per array
  • 1.5 mm height; 0.650 mm base width

Acustent Deployment

The Acustent device is deployed via an intuitive catheter-based system. The stent is kept in an housing, which contains protective lanes that ensure microneedles are undamaged during the deployment process.
